Current generic style names (Modern, Minimalist, Scandinavian, Industrial, Mid-Century Modern, Bohemian) undermine premium positioning — they read as IKEA catalog categories, not luxury real estate language.

The replacement strategy: name styles after the luxury lifestyle/context they evoke, not the design movement they reference.

Final 6 proposed names and their replacements:
- "Estate Modern" → replaces Modern
- "Collector's Reserve" → replaces Mid-Century Modern
- "Riviera Residence" → replaces Scandinavian
- "Atelier Noir" → replaces Industrial
- "Sanctuary" → replaces Minimalist
- "Grand Tour" → replaces Bohemian

**Why:** UHNW buyers respond to aspiration and lifestyle identity, not design taxonomy. A hedge fund manager buying in Bel Air wants to imagine their life in the space, not categorize it academically.

**How to apply:** When naming any buyer-facing UI element (styles, room types, service tiers), use lifestyle/aspirational language, not academic/design-school vocabulary. See [[project-vision]] for brand references.
